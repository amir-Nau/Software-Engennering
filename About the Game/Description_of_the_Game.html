Creator of the Game

The Mario game was first created by Shigeru Miyamoto. Shigeru Miyamoto is a Japanese video game designer for Nintendo company. He was once known as the "Steve Jobs" of video games.Prior to embarking on a career to video game design, he first started as an artist for Nintendo company in 1977 spanning over four decades which will lead him to become the father of video games.Miyamoto made his debut video game in December 1979 with an arcade game, "Radar Scope", release in Japan and in November 1980 worldwide. Before he created the Mario game, Miyamoto created games like Donkey Kong. After the success of Donkey King game series, he finally created a platform game 'Mario Bros' and this was based on the Jumpman character from Donkey Kong. Miyamoto is also the "Creative Fellow" of Nintendo Company. In 1997, he became the first video game developer to be inducted to Hall of Fame for "Academy of Interactive Arts and Science".

How the Game is Played

In Super Ghost Mario the controls are: left arrow for (left), right arrow for (right), down arrow for (crouch), up arrow for (jump), and 'A' key for (run/shoot). In this demo, you play as Mario who is stuck in limbo and must complete all 8 stages to be free from the curse. Good luck on the game.

About Mario

Mario is a Nintendo video game character, created by Shigeru Miyamoto. Mario is the all time mascot of the Nintendo. He's a human with mustache, wearing red trademark hat with letter M in front, blue overalls,white gloves, red shirt and brown shoes. Mario was known as "Jumpnan" in the classic arcade game Donkey Kong. He joined his brother, Luigi, in 1983 game, the newly christened Mario Bros.Besides the video game world, Mario also appeared in other media, for example, "The Super Mario Bros. Super Show!", and its sequel, "The Adventures of Super Mario Bros.3" as well as the live action 1993 film, "Super Mario Bros". Not only in video games, Mario also featured in merchandising products like calculators, clothes, hats, watches, cereal boxes, etc.
